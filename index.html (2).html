How Trustly works

Trustly is a neutral platform built on real buyer experiences — not accusations. Our goal is to make Rwanda's online marketplace safer for everyone: buyers and honest sellers alike.

🔍

1. Search any seller

Search by phone number, @handle, business name, or website domain to instantly see what other buyers experienced.

Shakisha umucuruzi ukoresheje nimero, izina, cyangwa website.

✍️

2. Write a review

Describe your experience factually. Upload payment proof or screenshots to strengthen your review.

Tanga ubuhamya bwawe nyakuri, wongere amashusho y'ingurane.

🛡️

3. Moderation first

Every review is checked by our team before publishing. Insults and unverifiable claims are removed — only facts.

Buri buhamya bugenzurwa mbere yo gushyirwa ahagaragara.

✅

4. Seller verification

Sellers can apply for a verified badge by submitting business registration or national ID. This builds buyer confidence.

Abacuruzi bashobora gusaba akamenyetso k'ukuri.

💬

5. Business responses

Verified sellers can respond to reviews publicly. Responding professionally to even negative reviews improves your trust score.

Abacuruzi bashobora gusubiza ubuhamya bwabo.

📊

6. Trust Score

Each business gets a score from 1–10 based on ratings, delivery rate, response rate, and verified transaction proofs.

Amanota y'icyizere abarwa buri munsi.

Community Rules / Amategeko

✅
Allowed: Factual accounts of what happened, dates, amounts, product descriptions, delivery outcomes.
✅
Allowed: Screenshots of payment confirmations, chats, or receipts (personal info blurred).
✅
Allowed: Positive reviews — great experiences deserve recognition too.
❌
Not allowed: Personal insults, threats, hate speech, or tribal language.
❌
Not allowed: Reviews without any factual basis or payment proof for large claims.
❌
Not allowed: Fake reviews — positive or negative. Phone verification deters this.
